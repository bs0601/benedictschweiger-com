**The Truth**

Agencies sell complexity because complexity keeps the chequebook open.

---

**The Illusion of Expertise**

Many business owners hand over the keys to their own engines. They sign a contract with an external agency and watch the money disappear. The promise is freedom. The reality is a black box. You pay eight thousand pounds a month for a service you cannot see. You do not know the hours spent. You do not know the tools used. You do not know the strategy executed.

This model works for the agency. They charge a flat rate regardless of the output. They do not need to move fast. They do not need to innovate. They need to keep the retainer active. An agency sells a feeling of security. You feel safe because a team is watching over your numbers. But is anyone actually watching?

The uncomfortable truth is this: the agency's best financial outcome and your best business outcome are structurally opposed.

---

**What We Actually Paid For**

At ALTHERR, we paid £96,000 in a single year to an agency that could not tell us, when we asked directly, how many qualified leads our campaigns had generated in the previous month.

That is the number. Sit with it.

Ninety-six thousand pounds. The report arrived every Friday. It was full of charts we did not understand, benchmarks we had not agreed to, and language that seemed designed to make the question feel impolite. We signed the invoice anyway. Every month. For twelve months.

When we finally pulled the contract and built the replacement ourselves, the entire operation cost us under £400 a month to run. We saw that saving within six weeks of going live. Not six months. Six weeks. The infrastructure was already there inside our own business. We had just been paying someone else to stand between us and it.

The mechanism matters less than the outcome, but since you will ask: we connected our own tools, built the workflows ourselves, and routed everything into a single dashboard we control entirely.* There is no middleman to filter what we see. The leads arrive the second they come in. The revenue is visible the second it lands. Nothing is interpreted for us before we see it.

That shift — from interpreted data to raw data — changed how fast we could move.

---

**The Speed of Autonomy**

The counterintuitive thing is that doing it yourself is actually faster.

We used to wait three days for the agency to reply to an email. We needed a change. We wanted to update a landing page. We sent a request. We waited for their internal meeting. We waited for their approval. We waited for the developer to start the task. By the time the change went live, the market had shifted.

Now we make the change ourselves in the same afternoon. No approval chain. No internal meeting. No waiting.

---

**Why Agencies Build This Dependency Deliberately**

Agencies thrive on information asymmetry. They know the tools. You do not. They know the algorithms. You do not. They use this gap to charge a premium. They call themselves specialists. In reality, they are intermediaries. They take your money and pass it on to freelancers or junior staff. The margin is theirs. The risk is yours.

They have no incentive to close the knowledge gap. If you understood the tools, you would not need them. So they present the tools as complex. They present the data as requiring interpretation. They present the workflow as requiring oversight. None of this is entirely false. But none of it is the whole truth either.

When you build in-house, you force clarity. You have to understand the logic of your own business. You cannot hide behind a monthly fee. If a workflow breaks, you fix it. If a campaign underperforms, you respond immediately. This ownership creates a sharper operation. It removes the layers of process that slow down decisions.

The agency model is designed for stability, not speed. They prefer a predictable monthly income to a variable performance outcome. They want you dependent. If you leave, they lose revenue. If you stay, they keep the status quo. They sell the problem to sell the solution.

---

**What Ownership Actually Looks Like**

This is not a manifesto against external help. There are good agencies. There are situations where they are the right call, particularly in early stages when internal capability is genuinely absent.

But if you are running a business above five million in annual revenue, you have the resources to own your own data infrastructure. You are not too small. You are too comfortable.

The question is not whether you can afford to build in-house. The question is whether you can afford to keep paying for the black box.

---

**The Broken Section**

Here is where most posts would give you a five-step framework. A checklist. A clean process that makes the transition feel manageable and safe.

We are not going to do that.

Because the real barrier is not process. It is the identity shift. You have to stop thinking of yourself as someone who needs an agency. You have to start thinking of yourself as someone who runs a data operation that also sells watches, or jewellery, or premium goods. The moment that shift happens, the question of whether to pay eight thousand pounds a month for someone else to interpret your own numbers becomes genuinely absurd.

That shift does not come from a checklist.

---

**FAQ**

**How long does it actually take to rebuild in-house?**
At ALTHERR it took six weeks from decision to full operation. That includes the time we spent second-guessing ourselves in week two. If you move with intent, four weeks is realistic. The technical side is faster than you expect. The psychological side takes longer.

**What if we do not have technical staff?**
You do not need a developer for most of this. Modern automation tools are built for operators, not engineers. If you can use a spreadsheet and describe a business process in plain English, you can build the core of what you need. The complexity agencies project onto this work is largely self-serving.

**Are there things agencies genuinely do better?**
Yes. Creative direction at volume. Influencer relationships at scale. Certain paid media buying where the agency has genuine platform access that an individual account would not. But these are specific, scoped tasks. They are not reasons to hand over your entire operation and lose visibility into the numbers.

**What does it actually cost?**
Our in-house system runs at under £400 a month in tooling costs. Against a previous retainer of £8,000 a month, that is a saving of ninety-one thousand pounds a year. Your numbers will differ. But the order of magnitude will likely surprise you.
