# Workbook v2.0 and v3.0 — Saved Ideas

*Archived from v1.0 cleanup on 2026-06-17. These placeholders were removed from the shipped workbook but preserved here for future versions.*

## v2.0 — Templates & Tools (Medium Lift)

### Listen Phase
1. **Customer Feedback Survey Template** — A proven ~12 question structure based on ALTHERR's running survey (1,978 responses). Covers discovery, brand association, service quality, NPS, reasons for not buying.
2. **Customer Data Capture Schema** — The specific data points to capture during Listen that fuel personalization in Convert. Examples: pet name/breed/birthday; watch reference/occasion/preference.
3. **Pattern Extraction Agent** — Claude Project setup for processing open-ended survey responses into themes, language patterns, and segment signals.
4. **Customer Truth Document Template** — The living artifact structure that captures what Listen reveals, referenced by every subsequent phase.

### Reach Phase
5. **Topic Battery Template** — A worksheet for identifying the first 16 high-intent topics from Customer Truth data.
6. **Brand Voice Configuration** — Claude Project setup with brand voice, customer language, and editorial rules pre-loaded.

### Capture Phase
7. **Capture Mechanism Selector** — Decision tree for picking the right capture mechanic (content gate vs. exclusive series vs. community access) for your market.
8. **List Segmentation Schema** — How to segment from day one so personalization scales later.

### Convert Phase
9. **Welcome Series Template** — 3-message welcome series with brand voice variables.
10. **Abandoned Cart Flow** — WhatsApp + email versions, with AI personalization layer.
11. **Inbound Response Agent** — Claude Project for classifying and drafting responses to customer inbound messages.

### Measure Phase
12. **Scorecard Template** — Google Sheets template with the six numbers and data source mappings.
13. **Monday Briefing Agent** — Claude Project that reads your scorecard and tells you what to do this week.

## Notes on What Was Removed

The v1.0 workbook shipped with 12 placeholder boxes (dashed borders, "Placeholder" label). These created a visual "unfinished" signal that undermined credibility. The content descriptions were strong — the ideas are preserved above — but the presentation promised deliverables that didn't exist.

v1.0 now focuses on:
- The framework explanation (what to do and why)
- The workspace (fill-in fields for your business)
- The checklist (actionable steps per phase)
- The "Do This Next" callouts (immediate action)
- The agent prompts (copy-paste ready, populated from workspace)

Templates and tools become the upsell path to v2.0 or the paid program.
