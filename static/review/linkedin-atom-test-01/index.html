Review: Scalability is not a goal

Slug: linkedin-atom-test-01

📋 Post
Scalability is not a goal. It's a trade-off. Six months into building an agentic content system, HeyGen was on the evaluation list. AI video avatars. One recording session, and you could produce as many videos as you wanted, personalised, at scale. The obvious move for a system designed to run without constant manual input. But I rejected it. Not because the technology is bad. HeyGen is impressive. I rejected it because AI-generated presence doesn't clear the quality bar that human presence sets. Viewers don't consciously identify what's wrong. They just feel slightly less connected. The micro-expressions aren't quite right. The eyes don't quite land. The trust signal is diluted. Not broken. Just off. And in content where the mechanism is trust, where the point is to build a relationship before a purchase decision, diluted is not good enough. The cost isn't reach. Reach can recover. The cost is credibility. You spend months building an audience that trusts you as a person, and a few AI-generated videos start to erode exactly what you built. So the camera stayed. The system does everything else. But this forced a harder question, one I think most people building AI-augmented content haven't answered yet: if the thing that cannot be automated is you in front of a camera, what does the rest of the system actually need to do to justify that constraint?
